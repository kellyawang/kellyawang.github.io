--- layout: details workTitle: Portraits id: portraits workDetails: - label: DATE data: December 2015 - label: DIMENSIONS data: 24 x 32 inches - label: MEDIA data: Ink Wash, Graphite Pencil summary: - line: There’s something melancholy about a stiff collared shirt tossed aside, lying crumpled on the floor. Wearing a collared shirt makes me feel confident. It reassures the world that I’ve “got everything together,” even when I really, really don’t. What do we suffer from hiding our hearts -- our emotive cores -- under a collared shirt? In my piece, the shirts themselves become artifacts of our insecurity, surrogates for a human body afflicted by emotional unrest. - line: I created this piece for a Studio Art course investigating the abstract and tangible properties that give certain objects power in our world. Through a process of experimentation and intellectual engagement with the objects that surround us, we developed artist statements (above) and created works for a gallery exhibition at Williams College. ---
