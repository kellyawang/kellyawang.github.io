Film/Animation


Illumination

October 2014

This short film features a procedurally generated city illuminated by neon lights designed, modeled, and rendered as part of our midterm project. I led the artistic team in developing a unified vision and worked especially closely with modeling and creating assets using Blender.

RE:new

December 2014

Desktop items come to life in this object animation short. The Cube, a new, technologically advanced device, enters into their midst, but even as it threatens to make the desktop characters obsolete, the next release of Cubes (Red) is already in production. I was responsible for framing each shot, taking clear and well-lit photos and sustaining the artistic vision that carried us through the project.



Steel Skull

October 2014

This short film was created for a lesson on rendering implicit surfaces using GLSL shaders I wrote and ran on the GPU. The skull I created as a whimsical response to the prompt. Each shot is composed with attention to camera angles and varying tempo of both subject and camera movement.

Color Rush Game Trailer

November 2014

This is a short trailer created for an infinite runner game we created in G3D. The player is a pink polyhedron rushing towards the end of the road to upbeat music and picking up as many points as possible along the way. I composed the visual environment of the game and worked especially on refining the player experience.

Paintings


Elephant

September 2014
20 x 26 inches
Oil Paint

This piece was done as a study on pointillism. A rich variety of intermediary colors appear through an optical mix between three primary colors mixed in darker or lighter shades. The repeated block-like and circular brushstrokes establish continuity between the foreground and background and the varying scale of objects in the frame, as well as brushstrokes used to model each form make this piece compelling.

Rom-Com Marc

December 2014
36 x 48 inches
Oil Paint

In this piece, our romantic hero peers kindly out at the viewers. The bright warm colors in the background and scarf are reflected in the layers of paint on the figure's face. The warm, friendly face and scarf pop out against the cool hues in the body and floor. Bold brushstrokes in the background and feet and extreme detail in the face always bring us back to the subject's face.
