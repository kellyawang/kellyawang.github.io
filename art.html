ARTISTIC PORTFOLIO


Animated Adventure Series (developed by Bit Films)

This past summer I had the incredible opportunity to work as a generalist and technical artist on a proof-of-concept test for an animated adventure series being developed at Bit Films, an independent animation studio in Central Massachusetts. Seeing firsthand each step of the production pipeline gave me valuable insight into how to work on a production team and establish efficient workflow under a tight deadline. I thoroughly enjoyed participating in daily reviews and learning how to pinpoint exactly what was not working in certain shots, and how to make it better.

In all materials, I had primary responsibility for creating digital environment art and prop design, before sending it down the pipeline for final editing and compositing.

Original Short Films

All films were made during the CS371: Computational Graphics course at Williams College in the Fall of 2014. Videos were created and rendered using the open-source C++ G3D Innovation Engine with original cinematography and artistic design and open-source music.

Illumination

October 2014

Special honors: Williams College Ward Prize for Best Project in Computer Science (2015)

This short film features a procedurally generated city illuminated by neon lights designed, modeled, and rendered as part of our midterm project. I led the artistic team in developing a unified artistic vision and also was responsible for modeling and creating 3D assets.



For behind the scenes, check out our presentation slides!

RE:new

December 2014

Special honors: Williams College Movie Madness Award for Best All-Around Film (2015)

Desktop items come to life in this object animation short. The Cube, a new, technologically advanced device, enters into their midst, but even as it threatens to make the desktop characters obsolete, the next release of Cubes (Red) is already in production. This was my first stop animation movie. I was responsible for framing each shot, taking clear and well-lit photos and sustaining the artistic vision that carried us through the project.

For behind the scenes, check out our presentation slides!



Steel Skull

October 2014

This short film was created for a lesson on rendering implicit surfaces using GLSL shaders I wrote and ran on the GPU. The skull I created as a whimsical response to the prompt. Each shot is composed with attention to camera angles and varying tempo of both subject and camera movement.

Color Rush Game Trailer

November 2014

This is a short trailer created for an infinite runner game we created in G3D. The player is a pink polyhedron rushing towards the end of the road to upbeat music and picking up as many points as possible along the way. I composed the visual environment of the game and worked especially on refining the player experience.

Paintings


Elephant

September 2014
20 x 26 inches
Oil Paint

A rich variety of intermediary colors appear in this piece, through an optical mix between three primary colors mixed in darker or lighter shades. The block-like and circular paintstrokes establish continuity between the foreground and background. In the spirit of pointilist art, brushstrokes are layered to convey a shimmering sense of volume and form.

Rom-Com Marc

December 2014
36 x 48 inches
Oil Paint

In this piece, our romantic hero peers kindly out at us. The bright warm colors in the background and scarf are reflected in the layers of paint on the figure's face. Bold brushstrokes in the background and feet stand as a contrast to the extreme detail in the face and the scarf.

Architecture


Light Box - Public Gallery and Cafe

August 2011
8 x 29 ¼ x 11 ½ inches
Chipboard composite, Basswood, Scale Figurines, Scale Model Car, Floral Arrangement


This piece was a design proposal created over the summer at the Pre-College program at the Rhode Island School of Design. The primary element in my model is a transparent floating mass that lets light into the central space and balances the visual weight of the opaque blocks around it. A linear walkway intersects the building, lifting foot traffic above the street behind the building, to the smaller mass behind it.

Cube

August 2011
4 x 4 x 4 inches
White Museum Board

These are two views of a sculpture I constructed over the summer during the Pre-College program at the Rhode Island School of Design. With this piece in particular, I enjoy the contrast between the simplicity of a cube and the complexity in the interior space of the cube, and each of its faces. No two faces are alike. Ribbons of space created by the window-like apertures and parallel lines across each face unify the overall structure.
