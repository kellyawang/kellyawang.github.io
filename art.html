ARTISTIC PORTFOLIO



Animation

Animated Adventure Series for Kids

This past summer I had the incredible opportunity to intern at Bit Films, an independent animation studio in Central Massachusetts. I worked as a generalist and digital artist on a proof-of-concept test for an animated adventure series currently being developed at Bit Films. In all materials, I had primary responsibility for creating digital environment art and prop design, before sending it down the pipeline for final editing tweaks and compositing. Over the course of the summer, I also edited together each sequence of the progressing film for daily team reviews. This film combined the 2D and 3D worlds of animation by overlaying 3D animated characters with flat, two-toned shading on top of 2D painted backgrounds.

Digital Film

All films were made during the CS371: Computational Graphics course at Williams College in the Fall of 2014. Videos were created and rendered using the open-source C++ G3D Innovation Engine with original cinematography and artistic design and open-source music.

RE:new

December 2014
Special honors: Williams College Movie Madness Award for Best All-Around Film (2015)
Click here for presentation slides and full project documentation! For more in depth documentation, see our project journal.
For best results please watch in HD

Desktop items come to life in this object animation short. The Cube, a new, technologically advanced device, enters into their midst, but even as it threatens to make the desktop characters obsolete, the next release of Cubes (Red) is already in production. This was my first stop animation movie, which I completed as part of a final group project. I was responsible for directing cinematography, framing each shot, taking clear and well-lit photos, and sustaining the artistic vision that carried us through the project.

Illumination

October 2014
Special honors: Williams College Ward Prize for Best Project in Computer Science (2015)
Click here for presentation slides and full project documentation! For detailed documentation, see our developer's journal
For best results please watch in HD

This short film features a procedurally generated city illuminated by neon lights designed, modeled, and rendered as part of a midterm group project. I was responsible for modeling and creating 3D models that were used as primitives in an original grammar that generated the buildings. I also led the team in developing a unified artistic vision that carried through to the final film.
Our group made strategic decisions for the two week span of the project in deciding to split up into teams that focused on creating the basic primitives, generating each building, generating an entire city, and writing scripts and an interface to manage the entire process. Because each building file was so massive, we needed to strategize when we were defining spline curves for the making of the video, and thus created a method for defining splines in a proxy city made up of simple rectangles, and then transferring the camera movements to the real city. Having responsive and dedicated team members allowed us to recognize when we needed to scale down our efforts in certain areas, and how to stay on track and complete each component of the project in a timely manner.



Steel Skull

October 2014

This short film was created for a lesson on rendering implicit surfaces using GLSL shaders I wrote and ran on the GPU. The skull I created as a whimsical response to the prompt. Each shot is composed with attention to camera angles and varying tempo of both subject and camera movement.




Paintings


Elephant

September 2014
20 x 26 inches
Oil Paint

A rich variety of intermediary colors appear in this piece, through an optical mix between three primary colors mixed in darker or lighter shades. The block-like and circular paintstrokes establish continuity between the foreground and background. In the spirit of pointilist art, brushstrokes are layered to convey a shimmering sense of volume and form.

Rom-Com Marc

December 2014
36 x 48 inches
Oil Paint

In this piece, our romantic hero peers kindly out at us. The bright, warm colors in the background and scarf are reflected in the layers of paint on the figure's face. Bold brushstrokes in the background and near the feet stand as a contrast to the extreme detail in the face and the scarf.




Drawings


Portraits

December 2015
24 x 32 inches
Ink Wash, Pencil

There’s something melancholy about a stiff collared shirt tossed aside, lying crumpled on the floor. Wearing a collared shirt makes me feel confident. It reassures the world that I’ve “got everything together,” even when I really, really don’t. Collared shirts act as a covering we wear over our upper bodies, but they also cover our hearts: our emotive cores. What do we suffer from hiding ourselves under a collared shirt? In my piece, the shirts themselves become artifacts of our insecurity, surrogates for the human body afflicted by inner emotional unrest.

I created this piece for a Studio Art course investigating through art the abstract and tangible properties that give certain objects power in our world. Through a process of experimentation and intellectual engagement with theories about the objects that surround us, we developed our artist statements (above) and created works that were then shown in a gallery exhibition at Williams College.
