Rethinking the Williams Dining Experience


This project was part of a Winter Study class on Design Thinking following the model taught at the Stanford d.school. We wanted to augment the Williams Dining experience by identifying what students want and need most in their dining hall experience. See presentation slides here.

User Point of View:

Vegetarians who eat at a single dining hall need to feel more sustained and satisfied by a varied vegetarian menu because meal options are often repetitive and leave diners hungry.

Design Questions:

How might we provide a more healthy and varied meal option for vegetarian students at Williams? ￼How might we make a vegetarian dish that is filling, but not repetitive? ￼How might we leverage the flexibility of an ingredient to offer distinctly different meals? How might we use the similarities between vegetarian and non-vegetarian meals to increase meal variety from the same resources?
