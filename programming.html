Computer Generated Film

All films were made during the CS371: Computational Graphics course at Williams College in the Fall of 2014. Videos were created and rendered using the open-source C++ G3D Innovation Engine with original cinematography and artistic design and open-source music.

Illumination

October 2014
Special honors: Ward Prize for Best Project in Computer Science (2015)
Click here for presentation slides and full project documentation

This short film features a procedurally generated city illuminated by neon lights designed, modeled, and rendered as part of our midterm project. I led the artistic team in developing a unified vision and worked especially closely with modeling and creating 3D assets.

Steel Skull

October 2014
Click here for full project documentation

This short film was created for a lesson on rendering implicit surfaces using GLSL shaders I wrote and ran on the GPU. The skull I created piece by piece as a whimsical response to the prompt. Each shot is composed with attention to camera angles and varying tempo of both subject and camera movement.



Color Rush Game Trailer

November 2014
Click here for gameplay documentation week 1 and week 2.

This is a short trailer created for an infinite runner game we created in G3D. The player is a pink polyhedron rushing towards the end of the road to upbeat music and picking up as many points as possible along the way. I composed the visual environment of the game and worked especially on refining the player experience. Week 1 was spent implementing user interaction with placeholder game pieces. Week 2 was completing implementation and finalizing design.
